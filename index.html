The Power of Compassion

Compassion is the simple decision to notice another person’s pain and lean toward it instead of away from it. It begins with attention: looking up from our screens, hearing the tremble in a voice, observing the neighbor whose lights are off more nights than usual. From attention grows understanding. We ask a small question—“Are you okay?”—and then we wait for the answer.

In that pause, trust starts to form. Compassion is not pity or a quick fix; it is presence. It says, “I see you,” and it stays long enough for dignity to return.

Acts of compassion are often quiet. A driver lets another merge and the road softens. A student shares notes and a classmate stays in school. A stranger covers the cost of medicine and a family breathes again. None of these moments trend, yet each alters the direction of a day. Compassion also requires courage. We may not solve every problem, but we can carry part of the weight.
